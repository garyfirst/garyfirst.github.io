Workshop Chair

  • Shili Ge, Guangdong University of Foreign Studies, China, geshili@gdufs.edu.cn
  • Shili Ge is a professor in applied linguistics and the director in Laboratory of Language and Artificial Intelligence, Guangdong University of Foreign Studies (GDUFS). He received his MA from Dalian Maritime University and PhD from Beijing Language and Culture University. His research interests include artificial intelligence in language learning and testing, automated translation quality assessment, and language comparative study for NLP. Before joining GDUFS, he was a professor at the School of Foreign Languages of South China University of Technology (SCUT). He has been teaching English, Statistics, Research Method, Introduction to Language Service, Web Localization and Computer Aided Translation to undergraduate and graduate students in the two universities for more than 10 years. He has over 40 publications in journals, conference proceedings and books, including NAACL, CCL, SETE, CWMT, Journal of Chinese Information Processing, and Journal of Modern Foreign Languages. He is the Co-Chair of all International Workshop on Educational Technology for Language (and Translation) Learning (ETLTL 2018 & 2019, ETLL 2020, 2021 & 2022) and the 1st and 2nd International Workshop on Discourse Processing (IWDP 2018 & 2020).

 

Workshop Co-Chairs

  • Martin Weisser, Guangdong University of Foreign Studies, China
  • Martin Weisser began his academic career in 1989 at the University of Wuumlrzburg, Germany, where he studied for an MA, with a one-semester break for a sholarship at Sheffield University, UK, courtesy of the German Academic Exchange Service (DAAD). He received his MA degree in English Linguistics (major), English Literature (1st minor), and Galloroman Philology (2nd minor) in 1994, and then began the first part of his PhD studies (primarily data collection) there. In October 1995, he moved to the University of Lancaster, UK, to take up a short PhD scholarship, awarded again by the DAAD. The following year, he decided to continue the PhD in Lancaster, soon switched to part-time status, and finally completed the degree in July 2001. After completing his PhD, he worked on a project with Geoffrey Leech in the Department of Linguistics, which ended at the end of September 2002, when he returned to Germany. After a brief stint in the former Department of Computational Linguistics at Erlangen University (January 2003 – September 2004), he moved to the Department of English Language and Linguistics at Chemnitz University of Technology, where he worked until January 2009. From April 2009 – January 2010, he substituted for the W2 chair in English Language and Linguistics at the University of Bayreuth, where he also submitted his professorial thesis (Habilitationsschrift) in June 2010. From July 2010 to June 2013, he was based in the Department of English at City University of Hong Kong, where he worked on a corpus of data from Philippine call-centres, as well as continuing his research into the improvement of automated pragmatic annotation/analysis. In January 2011, he received his full professorial qualification (venia legendi) from the University of Bayreuth. From July 2013 to July 2014, he first worked in the School of English and Education at Guangdong University of Foreign Studies as a Visiting Professor, but in August 2014 moved to the National Key Research Center for Linguistics and Applied Linguistics within the University. In December 2014, he was formally appointed as Professor of Linguistics and Applied Linguistics in Foreign Languages.

  • Menglian Liu, Guangdong University of Foreign Studies, China
  • Menglian Liu is an associate professor in Educational Technology and the deputy director in Collaborative Innovation Center for Language Research & Service, Guangdong University of Foreign Studies (GDUFS). She received her MA and PhD from South China Normal University. She once worked as a Deputy Director of the Academic Affairs Office in Guangdong Teachers College of Foreign Language and Arts. Her research interests include Educational Technology for Foreign Language Learning and Teaching, Educational Technology for Preschool Education, Technology Enhanced Instructional Design, Learning Technology and so on. She has been teaching Educational Technology for English Education, Educational Technology for Translation an Interpreting Education, Educational Technology for Preschool Education, General Psychology, Educational Research Method and College English to undergraduate and graduate students for more than 10 years. She has over 20 publications in journals, conference proceedings and books, including Chinese Translators Journal, Shanghai Journal of Translators, the Journal of Technology Enhanced Foreign Languages, the Journal of Modern Educational Technology, and Journal of Modern Distance Education. She designed and developed the teaching website for the 2007 Guangdong Quality Courses and the 2009 National Quality Course "French Interpretation". As a second participant. She joined in a research project "Reform and Practice of French Interpretation Courses Based on Internet and Multimedia Environment" as the 2nd participant. The project won the First Prize of the 6th Guangdong Provincial Teaching Achievement Award in 2010.
